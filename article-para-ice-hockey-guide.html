Para Ice Hockey at Milano Cortina 2026

Everything you need to know about the most exciting team sport at the Winter Paralympics.

📅 Published: February 20, 2026 ✏️ Updated: March 1, 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read Sport Spotlight

If there's one sport that captures the raw intensity, teamwork, and pure adrenaline of the Winter Paralympics, it's Para Ice Hockey. Formerly known as sled hockey or sledge hockey, this fast-paced, full-contact sport has been a crowd favorite since making its Paralympic debut at the 1994 Lillehammer Winter Games. At Milano Cortina 2026, Para Ice Hockey promises to deliver some of the most electrifying moments of the entire Games.

What Is Para Ice Hockey?

Para Ice Hockey is an adapted form of ice hockey designed for athletes with physical impairments affecting their lower body. Instead of skating, players sit on double-blade sledges that allow them to glide across the ice. They use two short sticks — each with a pick end for propulsion and a blade end for handling and shooting the puck.

The game follows rules very similar to standard ice hockey, with teams of six players (including a goaltender) competing to score goals. Games consist of three 15-minute periods, with body checking, slap shots, and dramatic saves all part of the action. The result is a sport that is just as fast, physical, and exciting as its able-bodied counterpart — some would argue even more so.

A Brief History of Para Ice Hockey

The sport was developed in Stockholm, Sweden, in the early 1960s by a group of athletes who wanted to continue playing ice hockey after sustaining physical disabilities. They welded two regular ice hockey blades together under a metal frame to create the first sledge, and the sport was born.

Key milestones in the sport's history include:

  • 1969: The first international game was played between Sweden and Norway.
  • 1994: Para Ice Hockey made its Paralympic debut at the Lillehammer Winter Games, with Sweden winning the first gold medal.
  • 2006: The sport continued to grow in Turin, Italy — the last time the Winter Paralympics were held in Italy.
  • 2010–2022: The United States, Canada, and South Korea emerged as dominant forces, with the US winning gold at multiple Games.
  • 2026: Milano Cortina will host 8 teams competing for glory, with the host nation Italy hoping for a strong showing on home ice.

The Equipment

Para Ice Hockey requires specialized equipment that allows athletes to play at the highest level:

  • Sledge: A custom-fitted metal frame mounted on two hockey skate blades. The sledge sits the player about 15 cm off the ice surface, low enough for puck handling but high enough for mobility.
  • Sticks: Each player uses two short sticks (about 1 meter long). One end has metal picks for grip and propulsion on the ice, while the other end has a curved blade for shooting and passing the puck.
  • Protective Gear: Players wear full protective equipment, including helmets with face cages, shoulder pads, elbow pads, gloves, and shin guards attached to the sledge. The goaltender wears additional padding.
  • Puck: A standard ice hockey puck is used — the same one found in NHL games, weighing approximately 170 grams.

Eligibility and Classification

Para Ice Hockey is open to male and female athletes with physical impairments affecting the lower body. This includes athletes with:

  • Spinal cord injuries (paraplegia)
  • Lower limb amputations (single or double)
  • Cerebral palsy affecting the lower body
  • Other conditions affecting lower limb function

Unlike many Paralympic sports, Para Ice Hockey does not have multiple classification categories — all eligible players compete together in a single category, creating a dynamic and inclusive team environment where athletes with varying impairments play side by side.

Tournament Format at Milano Cortina 2026

The 2026 Para Ice Hockey tournament will feature 8 national teams competing over approximately 8 days. The format typically includes:

  1. Preliminary Round: Teams are divided into two groups of four. Each team plays every other team in their group once (3 games each).
  2. Quarterfinals: The top teams from each group advance to the knockout stage.
  3. Semifinals: Winners compete for a place in the gold medal game.
  4. Medal Games: The Bronze Medal Game and the Gold Medal Game take place on March 14, 2026, at Milan PalaSharp.

Key Teams to Watch

Several national teams are expected to be strong contenders for medals at Milano Cortina 2026:

  • 🇺🇸 United States: The perennial powerhouse of Para Ice Hockey. The US has won multiple Paralympic gold medals and is always the team to beat. Known for their speed, physicality, and depth of talent.
  • 🇨🇦 Canada: Consistently competitive, Canada has been a fixture on the medal podium at Paralympic and World Championship events. Their structured play and strong goaltending make them dangerous opponents.
  • 🇰🇷 South Korea: A rising force in the sport, South Korea delivered a stunning performance at the 2018 PyeongChang Games on home ice and has continued to develop their program.
  • 🇮🇹 Italy: As the host nation, Italy will have passionate home support. The Italian team has been investing heavily in their Para Ice Hockey program and will be determined to deliver a memorable performance in Milan.
  • 🇨🇳 China: China burst onto the scene at the 2022 Beijing Paralympics and continues to build a competitive program with strong government support.

Where to Watch Para Ice Hockey at Milano Cortina 2026

All Para Ice Hockey games will be played at Milan PalaSharp, an arena located in the heart of Milan. The urban setting brings an incredibly vibrant atmosphere, with games expected to attract large and enthusiastic crowds. The arena has been renovated to meet the highest accessibility standards, ensuring a welcoming experience for all spectators.

For those unable to attend in person, games will be broadcast live on television and streaming platforms. Check our schedule page for the full game timetable.

Why Para Ice Hockey Is a Must-Watch

Para Ice Hockey is often cited as one of the most exciting sports at the Winter Paralympics — and for good reason:

  • Speed and Intensity: The pace of play is relentless. Players propel themselves at high speeds across the ice, with body checks, slap shots, and breakaway goals creating non-stop action.
  • Teamwork: Like all team sports, the best teams succeed through chemistry, communication, and selfless play. The bonds between teammates are palpable.
  • Accessibility: Para Ice Hockey breaks down barriers by showcasing that disability does not diminish competitiveness. The athletes on the ice are elite competitors in every sense.
  • Drama: With only one medal event (a single tournament), every game matters. The pressure builds throughout the tournament, culminating in the gold medal game — often one of the most dramatic moments of the entire Winter Paralympics.

Whether you're a longtime fan or watching for the first time, Para Ice Hockey at Milano Cortina 2026 is guaranteed to leave you on the edge of your seat. Don't miss it.
