Venues & Accessibility

A comprehensive guide to the world-class venues and inclusive infrastructure at Milano Cortina 2026.

📅 Published: February 25, 2026 ✏️ Updated: March 1, 2026 ⏱️ 9 min read Travel & Venues

The 2026 Winter Paralympics will take place across some of the most iconic and beautiful locations in northern Italy. From the ancient Roman amphitheatre in Verona to the snow-capped peaks of Cortina d'Ampezzo, the venues chosen for Milano Cortina 2026 are as impressive as the athletes who will compete in them. Equally important, the organizing committee has made accessibility a top priority, ensuring that all venues, transportation, and host cities are welcoming to everyone.

Overview of Competition Venues

The 2026 Winter Paralympics spans six key locations across the Lombardy, Veneto, and Trentino-Alto Adige regions of northern Italy. This distribution creates a unique Games experience that showcases the diversity of Italian geography, culture, and hospitality.

Cortina d'Ampezzo — Para Alpine Skiing

Known as the "Queen of the Dolomites," Cortina d'Ampezzo is a world-renowned mountain resort in the Veneto region. This glamorous town has a rich Olympic history — it hosted the 1956 Winter Olympics and will welcome the world once again in 2026.

Para Alpine Skiing events will take place on the Tofana and Olympia delle Tofane slopes, which offer challenging terrain with steep gradients and technical turns. The runs drop through stunning Dolomite scenery, making it one of the most visually spectacular venues in Paralympic history.

Accessibility Features

  • Fully accessible spectator areas with wheelchair-designated seating and clear sightlines
  • Accessible shuttle services connecting Cortina town center to competition venues
  • Accessible restrooms and hospitality facilities at all spectator zones
  • Audio description services available for vision-impaired spectators
  • Clear multilingual signage with tactile and Braille options

Anterselva/Antholz — Para Biathlon & Para Cross-Country Skiing

Nestled in the Antholz Valley of South Tyrol (Trentino-Alto Adige), Anterselva is home to one of the world's most prestigious biathlon venues. Sitting at an elevation of approximately 1,600 meters, the Südtirol Arena regularly hosts Biathlon World Cup events and World Championships.

The venue's alpine setting provides ideal conditions for both Para Biathlon and Para Cross-Country Skiing, with well-maintained trails winding through pine forests and alpine meadows.

Accessibility Features

  • Purpose-built accessible viewing platforms along the course
  • Warm-up and indoor accessible facilities for spectators
  • Adapted transportation from nearby villages and towns
  • Volunteer assistance teams trained in disability awareness

Livigno — Para Snowboard

Livigno is a high-altitude resort town situated at 1,816 meters in the Lombardy Alps, close to the Swiss border. Known for its excellent snow conditions, duty-free shopping (it's a customs-free zone), and vibrant après-ski scene, Livigno provides the perfect setting for Para Snowboard events.

Athletes will compete on purpose-built courses featuring the thrilling jumps, berms, and rollers that make snowboard cross and banked slalom so exciting to watch.

Accessibility Features

  • Accessible course-side viewing areas
  • Heated indoor spectator lounges with accessible facilities
  • Shuttle service with wheelchair-accessible vehicles
  • Dedicated accessibility team on-site

Milan — Para Ice Hockey & Wheelchair Curling

Italy's largest and most cosmopolitan city hosts the two indoor sports: Para Ice Hockey at PalaSharp and Wheelchair Curling at the Milan Ice Arena.

Milan brings an entirely different energy to the Games — urban, diverse, and culturally rich. Spectators will have the chance to combine watching elite Paralympic athletes with exploring one of Europe's most famous cities, known for its fashion, gastronomy, art, and architecture.

PalaSharp (Para Ice Hockey)

PalaSharp is a multi-purpose arena that has been renovated and upgraded for the Games. Its location in central Milan ensures excellent public transportation connections, making it easily accessible for all spectators.

Milan Ice Arena (Wheelchair Curling)

The Milan Ice Arena provides an intimate setting for wheelchair curling, where spectators can be close to the action and appreciate the strategic nuances of this precision sport.

Milan Accessibility Features

  • Milan's metro system has been significantly upgraded with elevators, tactile pathways, and audio announcements at all Games-related stations
  • Both arenas feature full wheelchair accessibility, accessible seating, and sensory rooms
  • Accessible hotel partnerships across the city
  • Multilingual assistance and information centers
  • Rideshare and taxi services with wheelchair-accessible vehicle options

Verona Arena — Opening Ceremony

The Verona Arena is one of the most remarkable venues in the history of the Paralympic Games. Built in the 1st century AD, this extraordinarily well-preserved Roman amphitheatre can seat approximately 15,000 spectators and has hosted operas, concerts, and major events for centuries.

For the 2026 Winter Paralympics, it will serve as the stage for the Opening Ceremony on March 6. The combination of ancient Roman architecture and the modern celebration of Paralympic sport will create an unforgettable atmosphere.

Accessibility Features

  • Historic venue adapted with temporary ramps, elevators, and accessible seating
  • Priority entry points for wheelchair users
  • Sign language interpretation and audio description available during the ceremony
  • Accessible transportation connections from Milan, Venice, and other cities

Cortina Olympic Ice Stadium — Closing Ceremony

The Closing Ceremony will be held at the Cortina Olympic Ice Stadium on March 15, bringing the Games to a fitting close in the heart of the Dolomites. The venue provides a more intimate setting compared to the grand Verona Arena, creating a warm and emotional farewell to the Games.

Getting Around: Transportation Between Venues

One of the unique challenges and opportunities of the Milano Cortina 2026 Games is the multi-venue layout spread across northern Italy. The organizing committee has developed a comprehensive transportation plan:

  • High-Speed Rail: Italy's excellent train network connects Milan to Verona (approximately 1.5 hours), making it easy to travel between ceremony and competition venues.
  • Dedicated Shuttle Services: Free shuttle buses will connect key hubs (Milan, Cortina, Anterselva, Livigno) to competition venues, with all vehicles wheelchair-accessible.
  • Air Travel: Milan Malpensa and Milan Linate airports serve as the main international gateways, with Venice Marco Polo Airport also conveniently located.
  • Accessibility Guarantee: All official Games transportation is guaranteed to be fully accessible, with trained staff and assistive equipment available.

Accessibility Legacy

Beyond the Games themselves, Milano Cortina 2026 aims to create a lasting accessibility legacy for Italy. Improvements to urban infrastructure, public transportation, hospitality facilities, and cultural venues are designed to remain in place long after the Paralympic flame has been extinguished.

The organizing committee has worked with disability advocacy organizations to ensure that the Games not only meet international accessibility standards but exceed them — setting a new benchmark for future Paralympic and Olympic Games.
