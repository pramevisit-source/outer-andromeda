The Complete Guide to the 2026 Winter Paralympics

Everything you need to know about the Milano Cortina 2026 Winter Paralympic Games.

📅 Published: February 15, 2026 ✏️ Updated: March 1, 2026 ⏱️ 12 min read Featured Guide

The 2026 Winter Paralympic Games are upon us, and Milano Cortina is preparing to host one of the most spectacular editions of the Games in Paralympic history. From the snow-capped peaks of the Dolomites to the vibrant streets of Milan, Italy is rolling out the red carpet for the world's most inspiring winter athletes. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know — from the sports and schedules to the venues and how to follow the action from anywhere in the world.

When and Where Are the 2026 Winter Paralympics?

The Milano Cortina 2026 Winter Paralympic Games will take place from March 6 to March 15, 2026, with wheelchair curling competition starting two days earlier on March 4. The Games will be spread across multiple stunning venues in northern Italy, including the cities and towns of Milano, Cortina d'Ampezzo, Anterselva/Antholz, Livigno, and Verona.

This marks Italy's return to hosting the Winter Paralympics, following the hugely successful 2006 Turin Winter Paralympics. The country's rich sporting culture, world-class infrastructure, and legendary hospitality make it an ideal host for the global Para sport community.

The Opening and Closing Ceremonies

The Opening Ceremony will be held at the Verona Arena, one of the most iconic venues in the world. This remarkably well-preserved 2,000-year-old Roman amphitheatre will provide an awe-inspiring backdrop as the world's Para athletes parade in and the Paralympic flame is lit. It promises to be a celebration of sport, inclusion, and Italian culture.

The Closing Ceremony will take place at the Cortina Olympic Ice Stadium on March 15, bringing the Games to a memorable close amid the breathtaking Dolomite mountains.

How Many Sports and Events?

The 2026 Winter Paralympics will feature 79 medal events across 6 sports:

  1. Para Alpine Skiing — 30 events (Downhill, Super-G, Giant Slalom, Slalom, Super Combined) across standing, sitting, and vision-impaired categories
  2. Para Biathlon — 18 events (Sprint, Individual, Middle Distance) across standing, sitting, and vision-impaired categories
  3. Para Cross-Country Skiing — 20 events (Sprint, Middle Distance, Long Distance, Relay) across standing, sitting, and vision-impaired categories
  4. Para Ice Hockey — 1 event (8-team tournament)
  5. Para Snowboard — 8 events (Banked Slalom, Snowboard Cross) across three classification categories
  6. Wheelchair Curling — 2 events (Team and the debut Mixed Doubles)

Over 700 athletes from more than 45 countries are expected to compete, making this one of the largest Winter Paralympics in history.

Key Venues Across Italy

One of the unique features of the Milano Cortina 2026 Games is the distribution of events across multiple spectacular locations in northern Italy. Each venue brings its own character and charm:

  • Cortina d'Ampezzo — The "Queen of the Dolomites" hosts all Para Alpine Skiing events. This glamorous resort town, which hosted the 1956 Winter Olympics, offers some of the most challenging and scenic slopes in the world.
  • Anterselva/Antholz — A world-renowned biathlon venue in South Tyrol, hosting Para Biathlon and Para Cross-Country Skiing. The scenic Nordic arena sits at an elevation of 1,600 meters and has hosted multiple World Championships.
  • Livigno — A high-altitude town near the Swiss border, hosting Para Snowboard events. Known for its duty-free shopping and excellent snow conditions, Livigno sits at 1,816 meters above sea level.
  • Milan — PalaSharp — The vibrant Italian metropolis hosts Para Ice Hockey in a purpose-renovated urban arena, bringing the energy of city sports culture to the Games.
  • Milan — Ice Arena — Wheelchair Curling takes center stage at a modern ice facility in Milan.
  • Verona Arena — The magnificent Roman amphitheatre hosts the Opening Ceremony, creating a link between ancient Roman heritage and modern athletic excellence.

What's New at Milano Cortina 2026?

Several exciting developments make the 2026 edition particularly noteworthy:

  • Mixed Doubles Wheelchair Curling Debut: For the first time in Paralympic history, mixed doubles wheelchair curling will be a medal event. Teams of one male and one female athlete will compete in this fast-paced format, adding a new dimension to the curling program.
  • Historic Venues: The use of the Verona Arena for the Opening Ceremony represents one of the most unique and prestigious ceremony venues in Paralympic history.
  • Sustainability Focus: The organizing committee has committed to hosting carbon-neutral Games, with a strong emphasis on sustainability, legacy, and environmental responsibility.
  • Accessibility Leadership: Milano Cortina 2026 aims to set new standards for accessibility, not just at competition venues but across all host cities, creating a lasting legacy for inclusive infrastructure in Italy.

The Paralympic Classification System

One of the most important aspects of Paralympic sports is the classification system. Classification ensures fair competition by grouping athletes with similar functional abilities together. At the Winter Paralympics, classifications generally fall into three broad categories:

  • Standing: Athletes with physical impairments who compete on their feet, sometimes using adaptive equipment like outriggers or prosthetics.
  • Sitting: Athletes who compete seated — using sit-skis for snow sports or sledges for ice hockey. This category typically includes athletes with spinal cord injuries or lower limb impairments.
  • Vision Impaired: Athletes with varying degrees of visual impairment who compete with the assistance of sighted guides. Both the athlete and guide receive medals.

Para Snowboard uses a different system based on upper limb (SB-UL) and lower limb (SB-LL1, SB-LL2) classifications, while wheelchair curling and para ice hockey have their own eligibility criteria.

How to Watch the 2026 Winter Paralympics

The Games will be broadcast globally through various channels:

  • Television: Major broadcasters worldwide will carry live coverage. Check with your national Paralympic committee for local broadcasting partners.
  • Online Streaming: The International Paralympic Committee (IPC) and various streaming platforms will offer live and on-demand coverage.
  • Social Media: Follow the IPC and Milano Cortina 2026 on social media platforms for highlights, athlete stories, and behind-the-scenes content.
  • In Person: If you're attending, tickets are available through the official Milano Cortina 2026 ticketing platform.

The Impact and Legacy of the Winter Paralympics

The Winter Paralympics are far more than a sporting event — they are a powerful catalyst for social change. By showcasing the incredible abilities of Para athletes, the Games challenge perceptions about disability, promote inclusion, and inspire millions of people worldwide.

Milano Cortina 2026 aims to leave a lasting legacy in Italy and beyond through improved accessibility infrastructure, greater awareness of adaptive sports, and inspiration for the next generation of Para athletes. The organizing committee has committed to ensuring that venues and transportation systems upgraded for the Games will remain accessible for generations to come.
