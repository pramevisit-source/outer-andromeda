About ParaWinter 2026

Dedicated to bringing you the most comprehensive coverage of the 2026 Winter Paralympic Games in Milano Cortina, Italy.

Our Mission

ParaWinter 2026 was created with a singular purpose: to provide the world's most comprehensive, accessible, and engaging coverage of the 2026 Winter Paralympic Games. We believe that Paralympic athletes deserve the same level of attention, admiration, and media coverage as their Olympic counterparts.

Our mission is to educate, inform, and inspire audiences worldwide about the incredible achievements of Para athletes who compete at the highest levels of winter sports. Through detailed guides, up-to-date schedules, and in-depth articles, we aim to make the 2026 Winter Paralympics accessible to everyone.

About the 2026 Winter Paralympics

The Milano Cortina 2026 Winter Paralympic Games will take place from March 6 to 15, 2026, across stunning venues in Milano, Cortina d'Ampezzo, Anterselva/Antholz, Livigno, and Verona in Italy. This marks Italy's return to hosting the Winter Paralympic Games, with the country bringing its world-renowned passion, culture, and hospitality to the global stage.

With 79 medal events across 6 sports, the Games will showcase the extraordinary abilities of over 700 athletes from more than 45 countries. The 2026 edition will also feature the historic debut of mixed doubles wheelchair curling, adding even more excitement to an already packed program.

The 6 Paralympic Sports

⛷️

Para Alpine Skiing

Athletes race down mountain slopes in events including Downhill, Super-G, Giant Slalom, Slalom, and Super Combined.

🎯

Para Biathlon

Combining cross-country skiing with rifle shooting, testing endurance and marksmanship.

🎿

Para Cross-Country

Endurance-based Nordic skiing across varying distances and terrain challenges.

🏒

Para Ice Hockey

Fast-paced team sport played on sledges, one of the most popular Paralympic events.

🏂

Para Snowboard

Athletes compete in snowboard cross and banked slalom with thrilling head-to-head action.

🥌

Wheelchair Curling

Strategy and precision on ice, featuring team events and the new mixed doubles format.
